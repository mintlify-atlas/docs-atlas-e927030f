This guide provides comprehensive assembly instructions for SmartSpin2k. The assembly process varies depending on your hardware version (V2 Through-Hole or V3 Integrated PCB).

## Before You Begin

<Warning>
Ensure you have all required components before starting assembly. Missing parts will interrupt the build process.
</Warning>

### Required Tools

- Soldering iron (for V2)
- Multimeter for testing connections
- Heat gun or soldering iron for heat-set inserts
- Phillips screwdriver
- Hex key set (M3, M5)
- Wire strippers
- 3D printer for printed components

### Hardware Version Selection

SmartSpin2k is available in two primary hardware versions:

- **V2 - Through-Hole**: DIY-friendly design with hand-solderable components and readily available parts
- **V3 - Integrated PCB**: Manufactured PCB with SMT components, includes serial interface for Peloton bikes

## V2 Through-Hole Assembly

### PCB Assembly

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prepare the PCB">
    Order the PCB using the gerber files from the Hardware/V2 directory. Use the optional soldering jig to align through-hole components for easier soldering.
    
    <img src="/Pictures/solder_jig_assembly.jpg" alt="Soldering jig for component alignment" />
  </Step>

  <Step title="Install heat-set inserts">
    Install M3 x 5 x 4 heat-set inserts (5mm diameter) into the case halves:
    - Heat your soldering iron to approximately 200-220°C
    - Place insert on the designated hole
    - Apply gentle pressure until insert is flush with surface
    - Allow to cool completely before handling
    
    You'll need these inserts to replace wood screws with M3 screws for better durability.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Solder PCB components">
    Solder all through-hole components to the PCB according to the schematic. Take care to:
    - Verify component orientation before soldering
    - Use the soldering jig to keep components aligned
    - Check for cold solder joints
    - Test continuity with multimeter
  </Step>

  <Step title="Assemble internal gearbox">
    Install the 11:40 tooth gear ratio:
    - Print both the 11-tooth and 40-tooth gears from Hardware/Common Assets/Gears
    - For metal shaft option, print the 40T HEX version and use a 5/16" bolt
    - For plastic shaft, use the standard 40T gear with integrated shaft
    - Ensure gears mesh properly with minimal backlash
  </Step>

  <Step title="Install knob cup and insert">
    Select the appropriate knob insert for your bike from Hardware/Common Assets/Inserts:
    - Choose the insert matching your bike's resistance knob
    - Install the knob cup that retains the insert
    - The knob cup connects to the internal gearbox
    - Test fit on your bike's knob before final assembly
  </Step>

  <Step title="Assemble case halves">
    Join the left and right case halves:
    - Route wiring carefully to avoid pinching
    - Use 4x M3 screws to secure the halves together
    - Ensure all components fit properly before tightening
    - Verify the stepper motor shaft aligns with the gear
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Case Options

#### Direct Mount (Recommended)

The direct mount design provides superior stability, especially for bikes with spherical knobs.

**Requirements:**
- Minimum clearance specifications:
  - Long case: 115mm minimum between knob center and head tube (suits Bowflex C6, Peloton, Echelon Connect Sport)
  - Short case: 81mm minimum clearance (suits Bowflex C7)
- Additional components from Common Assets:
  - Arm (length depends on your bike - see mounting guide)
  - Bike mount (specific to your bike's head tube)

**Bill of Materials (Direct Mount):**
- M3 x 5 x 4 heat-set inserts (5mm diameter)
- 4x M3 screws (M3x8 or M3x10)
- 1x M5x12mm screw for the arm
- 2x M5x30mm screws (bike mount and case)
- 3x M5 nuts
- Optional: 1/2" wide velcro cable tie (8" length) for bike mount
- Optional: 2mm non-slip adhesive backed rubber for bike mount protection

#### Universal Mount

Suitable for bikes without a direct mount solution or when portability is desired.

**Requirements:**
- Minimum 49mm clearance between knob center and rear of head tube
- More portable but less rigid than direct mount

#### Ultra Short Mod

For bikes with less than 49mm head tube spacing, this shortened version of the V2 case allows installation in very compact spaces.

## V3 Integrated PCB Assembly

<Steps>
  <Step title="PCB preparation">
    The V3 PCB uses SMT components and should be manufactured by a PCB assembly service. Verify all components are properly soldered upon receipt.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Serial interface for Peloton">
    The V3 board includes a UART-to-serial driver for direct communication with Peloton bikes:
    - Locate the auxiliary serial connections (pins defined in settings.h:196-200)
    - Verify TX/RX pin assignments match your configuration
  </Step>

  <Step title="Assemble gearbox and case">
    Follow the same gearbox assembly process as V2:
    - Install 11:40 tooth gears
    - Add knob cup and bike-specific insert
    - Ensure rigid mounting to bike frame
  </Step>

  <Step title="Final assembly">
    Complete the V3 assembly with direct mount components:
    - Install the arm (see Arm selection guide)
    - Attach bike mount to head tube
    - Connect case to arm with M5 hardware
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Components Assembly

These components are required for both V2 and V3 builds:

### Gearbox Assembly

- 11-tooth pinion gear on stepper shaft
- 40-tooth driven gear connected to knob cup
- 3.636:1 gear ratio (11:40)
- Provides optimal torque and resolution for resistance control

### Shifters (Optional)

Print manual shifters from Hardware/Common Assets/Shifters in TPU for flexibility. These allow manual resistance adjustments during rides.

## Printing Instructions

For all 3D printed components:

- **Perimeters**: 4
- **Infill**: 40%
- **Layer height**: 0.2mm
- **Supports**: Enable from print bed only for case halves
- **Material**: PLA or PETG for structural parts, TPU for shifters

## Post-Assembly Testing

<Warning>
Do not connect to bike until all electrical tests pass. Incorrect wiring can damage components.
</Warning>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visual inspection">
    Check all solder joints, verify no shorts between traces, ensure all connectors are properly seated.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Continuity testing">
    Use a multimeter to verify:
    - Power connections (3.3V, 5V, GND)
    - Stepper motor connections
    - Serial communication lines
    - Button connections
  </Step>

  <Step title="Power-on test">
    Connect power and verify:
    - LED indicator lights (Pin 2)
    - ESP32 boots successfully
    - No smoke or unusual heat
    - WiFi access point appears
  </Step>

  <Step title="Mechanical test">
    Before mounting to bike:
    - Test stepper motor rotation
    - Verify gear engagement
    - Check for binding or unusual resistance
    - Test manual shifter buttons
  </Step>
</Steps>
