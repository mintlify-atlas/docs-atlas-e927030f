Proper mounting is critical for SmartSpin2k performance. A secure, rigid mount ensures accurate resistance control and prevents slippage during intense workouts.

## Mounting System Overview

The SmartSpin2k mounting system consists of three main components:

1. **Bike Mount**: Attaches to your bike's head tube
2. **Arm**: Connects the bike mount to the SmartSpin2k case (length varies by bike)
3. **Case Assembly**: Contains electronics and gearbox, connects to resistance knob

This modular design accommodates various bike geometries with minimal modification.

## Pre-Installation Measurements

<Steps>
  <Step title="Measure knob-to-head tube distance">
    Measure from the back of the head tube (side closest to you) to the center of the resistance knob.
    
    This measurement determines the required arm length:
    - Measured distance minus 65mm = approximate arm length needed
    - Example: 145mm measured - 65mm = 80mm arm
  </Step>

  <Step title="Check clearance requirements">
    Verify minimum clearances for your case type:
    
    **Direct Mount Long Case**:
    - Minimum 115mm knob-to-head tube clearance
    - Suitable for: Bowflex C6, Peloton Bike, Echelon Connect Sport
    - Uses arm length typically 65-105mm
    
    **Direct Mount Short Case**:
    - Minimum 81mm clearance
    - Suitable for: Bowflex C7
    - Uses shorter arms (45-65mm)
    
    **Universal Mount**:
    - Minimum 49mm clearance
    - More portable, less rigid
    
    **Ultra Short Mod**:
    - For bikes with less than 49mm clearance
    - Requires modified case design
  </Step>

  <Step title="Identify your bike's head tube profile">
    - Measure head tube width and shape (round, square, oval)
    - Check if existing bike mounts are available in Hardware/Common Assets/Bike Mount/
    - If not available, you may need to customize using the OpenSCAD model
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Installation Process

### Installing the Bike Mount

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose mounting method">
    Select your preferred mounting approach:
    
    **Option A: VHB Tape (Semi-Permanent)**
    - Clean head tube surface thoroughly
    - Apply VHB tape to bike mount contact surfaces
    - Press firmly and allow 24 hours to cure
    - Provides excellent strength without paint damage risk
    
    **Option B: Velcro Strap (Removable)**
    - Apply 2mm non-slip adhesive rubber to mount contact points
    - Position mount on head tube
    - Secure with 1/2" wide velcro cable tie (8" length for most bikes)
    - Protects paint finish, allows removal between rides
    - Tighten securely to prevent movement
  </Step>

  <Step title="Position bike mount">
    - Place mount on head tube at measured position
    - Ensure mount is level and properly aligned
    - Verify clearance for arm movement
    - Mark position before final attachment
  </Step>

  <Step title="Secure bike mount">
    - Apply chosen mounting method (VHB or strap)
    - Verify mount is solidly attached with no movement
    - Allow cure time if using adhesive
    - Test with moderate force before proceeding
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
A loose bike mount will cause erratic resistance control and may damage your bike's knob. Ensure the mount is completely secure before continuing.
</Warning>

### Installing the Arm

<Steps>
  <Step title="Attach arm to bike mount">
    - Use 1x M5x30mm screw through bike mount into arm
    - Add M5 nut and tighten securely
    - Arm should pivot freely but without excessive play
  </Step>

  <Step title="Attach arm to case">
    - Position arm hook on case mount point
    - Use 1x M5x12mm screw through arm into case
    - Add M5 nut and tighten
    - Verify arm angle allows knob engagement
  </Step>

  <Step title="Test arm movement">
    - Arm should rotate smoothly on both pivot points
    - No binding or excessive resistance
    - Sufficient range of motion for all resistance settings
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Installing the Knob Cup and Insert

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select correct insert">
    Choose the insert from `Hardware/Common Assets/Inserts/` that matches your bike:
    - Peloton
    - Echelon
    - Bowflex/Schwinn
    - Generic spherical
    - Generic cylindrical
    - Many other bike-specific designs available
  </Step>

  <Step title="Install insert into knob cup">
    - Press insert firmly into knob cup
    - Ensure insert is fully seated
    - Test fit on bike's resistance knob
    - Insert should grip knob firmly without slipping
  </Step>

  <Step title="Attach knob cup to gearbox">
    - Align knob cup shaft with internal gearbox
    - If using 40T HEX gear, insert 5/16" bolt through shaft
    - Secure with appropriate hardware
    - Ensure no wobble or play in connection
  </Step>

  <Step title="Mount to bike knob">
    - Position SmartSpin2k case near resistance knob
    - Slide knob cup insert onto bike knob
    - Ensure firm engagement with no slippage
    - Rotate knob by hand to verify smooth operation
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
If the insert slips on the knob during operation, resistance control will be inaccurate. You may need a different insert design or modifications for your specific knob shape.
</Warning>

### Final Case Mounting

<Steps>
  <Step title="Position case assembly">
    - With knob cup attached to bike knob, position case
    - Connect arm to case mount point
    - Use 1x M5x30mm screw through case mount
    - Add M5 nut and tighten securely
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify alignment">
    - Check that all connections are tight
    - Ensure case doesn't interfere with pedals or frame
    - Verify cables have sufficient slack for movement
    - Test full range of motion manually
  </Step>

  <Step title="Test installation">
    - Manually rotate resistance knob through full range
    - Listen for unusual noises (grinding, clicking)
    - Check for any binding or resistance
    - Verify all mounting hardware is secure
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Installation Verification

<Steps>
  <Step title="Mechanical inspection">
    - All M5 screws properly tightened
    - No wobble in any connection points
    - Knob cup grips resistance knob firmly
    - Gears mesh properly with minimal backlash
    - Case clears all bike frame components
  </Step>

  <Step title="Range of motion test">
    - Manually turn resistance from minimum to maximum
    - SmartSpin2k should track smoothly throughout
    - No binding at any point in range
    - Knob returns to set position when released
  </Step>

  <Step title="Powered test">
    - Connect power to SmartSpin2k
    - Use shifter buttons to adjust resistance
    - Verify stepper motor moves knob accurately
    - Check for any unusual vibration or noise
    - Confirm resistance changes match commands
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Mounting Troubleshooting

### Knob slippage

**Symptoms**: Resistance doesn't change when stepper moves, or changes inconsistently.

**Solutions**:
- Try a different insert design for your knob shape
- Add friction material (rubber, silicone) to insert contact points
- Verify knob cup is fully engaged with knob
- Check for worn or damaged insert

### Excessive vibration

**Symptoms**: Rattling or shaking during resistance changes or pedaling.

**Solutions**:
- Tighten all mounting hardware (M5 screws, M3 case screws)
- Verify bike mount is solidly attached to head tube
- Check gear mesh - should be snug but not binding
- Add dampening material between case and mount

### Limited range of motion

**Symptoms**: Cannot reach full resistance range, or hits mechanical stops prematurely.

**Solutions**:
- Verify correct arm length for your bike
- Check arm angles at both extremes of travel
- Ensure case doesn't interfere with frame at any position
- May need longer or shorter arm depending on geometry

### Case interference with frame

**Symptoms**: Case touches frame, handlebars, or other bike components.

**Solutions**:
- Adjust arm length (may need shorter or longer)
- Rotate bike mount on head tube for better clearance
- Use different case variant (short case, ultra-short mod)
- Verify bike compatibility - some geometries may not work

## Bike-Specific Mounting Notes

### Peloton Bike

- Use 75mm arm (90mm if handlebars are slammed)
- V3 PCB recommended for serial communication
- Connect auxiliary serial for direct resistance reading
- Peloton-specific insert available in Common Assets

### Bowflex C6 / Schwinn IC4

- Use 85mm arm
- IC4 bike mount design works for both models
- Square head tube profile
- Velcro strap mounting recommended for paint protection

### Echelon Connect Sport

- Use 105mm arm
- Echelon-specific bike mount available
- May require arm adjustment based on setup

### Bowflex C7

- Use short case variant
- 45mm arm length
- Tight clearances - verify fit carefully
- Limited space requires precise mounting

## Maintenance and Adjustments

### Periodic Checks

Perform these checks every 30 days or 20 hours of use:

- Tighten all mounting hardware (M5, M3 screws)
- Inspect insert for wear or damage
- Check gear mesh for proper engagement
- Verify bike mount hasn't shifted
- Test full range of motion

### Re-tensioning

If using velcro strap mounting:
- Check strap tension monthly
- Replace worn straps immediately
- Verify anti-slip rubber pads are intact

### Insert Replacement

Replace the knob insert if:
- Visible wear or cracking
- Slippage occurs during use
- Inconsistent resistance control
- Print a replacement from STL files in Common Assets
