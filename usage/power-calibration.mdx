## Why Calibrate Power?

Many spin bikes have inaccurate or non-existent power meters. SmartSpin2k offers two calibration methods:

1. **Power Multiplier** - Scale readings from an existing power meter
2. **Power Table** - Build a custom power curve for bikes without power meters

Accurate power data is essential for:
- Effective ERG mode training
- Tracking fitness progress over time
- Comparing workouts across different platforms
- Setting appropriate FTP (Functional Threshold Power)

## Method 1: Power Multiplier Calibration

<Info>
  Use this method if your bike has a built-in power meter that reads consistently high or low.
</Info>

### When to Use Power Multiplier

- Your bike has a power meter but it's inaccurate (e.g., consistently 10% high)
- You want to match power readings from a different bike or power meter
- You've verified your power meter is offset by a consistent percentage

### Calibration Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Establish baseline">
    Perform a controlled test ride and note the average power reported by your bike's power meter.
    
    Ideally, do a 20-minute FTP test or a steady 10-minute effort.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Compare to known value">
    If you have access to a calibrated power meter (from another bike or a power meter rental), perform the same test and compare.
    
    **Example**: 
    - Your bike reports 250W average
    - Calibrated meter reports 225W average
    - Your bike reads 11% high (250/225 = 1.11)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Calculate correction factor">
    Correction Factor = Actual Power ÷ Reported Power
    
    Using the example above:
    - Correction Factor = 225 ÷ 250 = **0.90**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Apply in SmartSpin2k">
    Navigate to: **Web Interface > Settings > Power Multiplier**
    
    Set the slider to your calculated correction factor (0.90 in this example).
    
    Range: 0.10x to 2.10x (default: 1.00x)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Verify calibration">
    Perform another test ride and confirm the power readings match your expectations.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Power Multiplier Settings

Access via: **Web Interface > Settings**

```javascript
// From settings.html:87-94
powerCorrectionFactor: {
  type: 'slider',
  title: 'Power Multiplier',
  tooltip: 'Power Meter Correction Factor',
  tooltipText: 'Adjust this to calibrate the output of your power meter.',
  min: 0.10, max: 2.10, step: 0.01,
  defaultValue: 1.00,
  unit: 'x'
}
```

<Tip>
  Changes to the Power Multiplier take effect immediately. You don't need to restart SmartSpin2k.
</Tip>

## Method 2: Power Table Calibration

<Info>
  Use this method if your bike doesn't have a power meter or you want to simulate power based on resistance and cadence.
</Info>

### How the Power Table Works

SmartSpin2k builds a lookup table that maps:

```
(Resistance Position, Cadence) → Estimated Power
```

As you ride at different resistance levels and cadences, SmartSpin2k records:
- **Stepper position** (resistance level)
- **Cadence** (RPM from sensor or power meter)
- **Power** (from connected power meter or user input)

Over time, this creates a comprehensive power curve for your specific bike.

### Building Your Power Table

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect a power meter (recommended)">
    For best results, temporarily connect a power meter to SmartSpin2k while building the table.
    
    If you don't have a power meter, you can manually input expected power values or use rough estimates.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Disable PowerTable For Power">
    **Important**: Initially, keep "PowerTable For Power" **OFF** in Settings.
    
    This allows SmartSpin2k to use your connected power meter to build the table.
    
    Settings > PowerTable For Power > OFF
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Ride at various intensities">
    Perform rides that cover a wide range of:
    
    **Cadence ranges**:
    - Low: 40-60 RPM
    - Medium: 60-90 RPM
    - High: 90-120 RPM
    
    **Power levels**:
    - Easy: 50-100W
    - Moderate: 100-200W
    - Hard: 200-300W
    - Very Hard: 300W+
    
    Spend at least 30-60 seconds at each combination.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Let the table populate">
    SmartSpin2k automatically records data points as you ride.
    
    The power table is saved every 10 seconds to `/PowerTable.txt` on the device.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Verify coverage">
    After 3-5 rides, you should have good coverage across the range of cadence and resistance you typically use.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enable PowerTable For Power (optional)">
    Once you have a well-populated table, you can enable "PowerTable For Power" in Settings.
    
    This makes SmartSpin2k use the power table instead of a connected power meter.
    
    <Warning>
      Only enable this if you don't have a power meter connected, or if you want to use the table instead of your power meter.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Power Table Data Structure

The power table uses the following resolution:

```cpp
// From Power_Table.h
#define POWERTABLE_CAD_INCREMENT 5   // 5 RPM bins
#define POWERTABLE_WATT_INCREMENT 5  // 5 Watt bins
```

**Cadence bins**: 40, 45, 50, 55, ... 120 RPM
**Power bins**: 0, 5, 10, 15, ... 2000W

For each `(cadence, power)` pair, the table stores the corresponding **stepper position**.

### Example Power Table Session

<CodeGroup>
```text Sample Ride Data
Time  | Cadence | Stepper Pos | Power | Action
------|---------|-------------|-------|------------------
00:00 | 80 RPM  | 1000        | 150W  | Recorded to table
01:00 | 80 RPM  | 1500        | 200W  | Recorded to table
02:00 | 90 RPM  | 1500        | 220W  | Recorded to table
03:00 | 70 RPM  | 1500        | 180W  | Recorded to table
04:00 | 85 RPM  | 2000        | 280W  | Recorded to table
```
</CodeGroup>

After this ride, SmartSpin2k knows:
- At 80 RPM, position 1000 = ~150W
- At 80 RPM, position 1500 = ~200W
- At 90 RPM, position 1500 = ~220W
- etc.

### Using the Power Table in ERG Mode

When ERG mode receives a target power command:

1. **Lookup**: SmartSpin2k queries the table for the stepper position needed to achieve the target power at the current cadence
2. **Move**: The stepper motor moves to that position
3. **Refine**: PID control makes small adjustments to hit the exact target

This allows for quick, accurate jumps to new power targets during interval training.

## Resetting the Power Table

You may need to reset the power table if:
- Your bike's resistance mechanism has been serviced or modified
- You've replaced brake pads or adjusted tension
- The power table produces inaccurate estimates
- You want to start fresh with new calibration data

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access developer tools">
    Navigate to: **Web Interface > Developer Tools**
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Reset power table">
    Look for the "Reset Power Table" option.
    
    <Warning>
      This will delete all recorded power table data. This action cannot be undone.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm reset">
    Confirm the reset action.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Rebuild the table">
    Follow the "Building Your Power Table" steps above to repopulate with new data.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Alternatively, reset via code:

```cpp
// From ERG_Mode.cpp:110-118
if (ss2k->resetPowerTableFlag) {
  LittleFS.remove(POWER_TABLE_FILENAME);
  powerTable->reset();
  userConfig->setHMin(INT32_MIN);
  userConfig->setHMax(INT32_MIN);
  spinBLEServer.spinDownFlag = 0;
  rtConfig->setHomed(false);
  userConfig->saveToLittleFS();
}
```

## Minimum and Maximum Watts

SmartSpin2k allows you to set minimum and maximum watt thresholds to protect your bike and ensure realistic power readings.

### Min Brake Watts

**Settings > Min Brake Watts** (Default: 0W, Range: 0-200W)

Sets the minimum power output before SmartSpin2k stops adjusting resistance.

- **Use case**: If your bike always produces at least 20W of resistance at the lowest setting, set this to 20W
- **0W = disabled**: No minimum power check

```cpp
// From ERG_Mode.cpp:163-166
// Set minimum set point to minimum bike watts
if (rtConfig->watts.getTarget() < userConfig->getMinWatts()) {
  SS2K_LOG(ERG_MODE_LOG_TAG, "ERG Target Below Minimum Value.");
  rtConfig->watts.setTarget(userConfig->getMinWatts());
}
```

### Max Brake Watts

**Settings > Max Brake Watts** (Default: 0W, Range: 0-2000W)

Sets the maximum expected power output from your bike.

- **Use case**: If your bike maxes out at 500W, set this to 500W to flag unrealistic power readings
- **0W = disabled**: No maximum power check

<Tip>
  Set Max Brake Watts to the highest power you've ever produced on your bike during a sprint or max effort.
</Tip>

## Spin Down Calibration

Some training apps (like Zwift) support spin-down calibration, similar to wheel-on trainers.

### How Spin Down Works

1. **App initiates**: The training app sends a `SpinDownControl` command via FTMS
2. **User accelerates**: You pedal up to a target speed (typically 8-24 km/h)
3. **Coast down**: Stop pedaling and let the bike coast to a stop
4. **Measure**: The app measures deceleration to estimate resistance

<Note>
  SmartSpin2k acknowledges spin-down commands but does not actively use the data for calibration, since it directly controls resistance via the stepper motor.
</Note>

### Performing a Spin Down in Zwift

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open pairing screen">
    In Zwift, go to the device pairing screen.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select controllable trainer">
    Ensure SmartSpin2k is paired as your controllable trainer.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click settings icon">
    Click the gear icon next to SmartSpin2k.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose 'Spindown'">
    Select the spindown option.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Follow instructions">
    Pedal up to the target speed, then coast.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Complete calibration">
    Zwift will confirm the spindown is complete.
  </Step>
</Steps>

```cpp
// From BLE_Fitness_Machine_Service.cpp:303-321
case FitnessMachineControlPointProcedure::SpinDownControl: {
  // Response: Target speeds 8.00 km/h and 24.00 km/h
  uint8_t responseParams[] = {0x20, 0x03, 0x60, 0x09};
  
  returnValue = {ResponseCode, SpinDownControl, Success};
  returnValue.insert(returnValue.end(), 
                     std::begin(responseParams), 
                     std::end(responseParams));
  
  spinBLEServer.spinDownFlag = 2;
}
```

## Comparing Calibration Methods

  <Card title="Power Multiplier" icon="calculator">

    - No data collection needed

    - Requires existing power meter

## Validating Your Calibration

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="FTP Test Comparison">
    Perform an FTP test (20-minute all-out effort) before and after calibration.
    
    Your FTP should be consistent with your perceived effort and heart rate.
    
    Typical FTP values:
    - Beginner: 1.5-2.0 W/kg
    - Intermediate: 2.5-3.5 W/kg
    - Advanced: 3.5-4.5 W/kg
    - Elite: 4.5+ W/kg
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Perceived Effort Check">
    At calibrated power levels:
    - 100W should feel very easy (conversational pace)
    - 200W should feel moderate (can talk in short sentences)
    - 300W should feel hard (difficult to talk)
    - 400W+ should feel very hard (max effort)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Heart Rate Correlation">
    If you use a heart rate monitor, check that power and HR correlate:
    
    - Low power (50-100W) → Low HR (Zone 1-2)
    - Moderate power (150-250W) → Moderate HR (Zone 3-4)
    - High power (300W+) → High HR (Zone 5)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Consistency Test">
    Ride the same workout multiple times and verify:
    - Average power is consistent (±5%)
    - Heart rate response is similar
    - RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion) matches
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Troubleshooting Calibration Issues

### Power reads too high

- **Decrease Power Multiplier**: Try 0.90x or 0.85x
- **Check power meter placement**: Ensure it's installed correctly
- **Verify cadence sensor**: Inaccurate cadence can affect power calculations

### Power reads too low

- **Increase Power Multiplier**: Try 1.10x or 1.15x
- **Recalibrate power meter**: Some power meters require zero-offset calibration
- **Rebuild power table**: Collect more high-power data points

### Inconsistent power readings

- **Check for loose components**: Tighten crank arms, pedals, and power meter
- **Replace power meter battery**: Low battery can cause erratic readings
- **Improve power table coverage**: Ride at more varied intensities
- **Reduce ERG Sensitivity**: High sensitivity can cause oscillations

### Power table not improving

- **Verify data is being saved**: Check `/PowerTable.txt` exists on device
- **Ensure power meter is connected**: Table needs power input to build
- **Ride at different resistance levels**: Cover full range of stepper positions
- **Reset and rebuild**: If table is corrupted, start fresh
