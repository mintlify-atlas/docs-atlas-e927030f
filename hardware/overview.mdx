# Hardware Overview

SmartSpin2k is available in multiple hardware versions, each designed for different use cases and skill levels. Choose the version that best fits your needs and capabilities.

## Current Versions

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="V3 - Integrated PCB (Latest)">
    ### V3 - Integrated PCB
    
    The newest and most advanced version with a fully integrated custom PCB.
    
    **Key Features:**
    - Custom manufactured PCB with SMT components
    - UART to serial driver for direct Peloton bike communication
    - Automated manufacturing capability
    - Rigid direct mount system
    - "Print, Plug, and Play" - minimal soldering required
    - 11:40 tooth gear ratio
    
    **Compatibility:**
    - Peloton Bike (with serial interface)
    - Bowflex C6
    - Echelon Connect Sport
    - Schwinn IC4
    - Other bikes with 80mm+ clearance
    
    **Skill Level:** Beginner to Intermediate
    
    **Required Clearance:** Minimum 80mm between knob center and head tube
    
    [Learn more about V3 →](/hardware/v3-integrated-pcb)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="V2 - Through Hole">
    ### V2 - Through Hole
    
    The DIY-friendly version with hand-solderable through-hole components.
    
    **Key Features:**
    - Hand-solderable carrier board PCB
    - Smaller, easier to print case design
    - Multiple case options (Direct Mount, Universal, Ultra Short)
    - Improved gear ratio over V1
    - Easy wiring with devkit mounting
    - 11:40 tooth gear ratio
    
    **Case Options:**
    - **Direct Mount (Recommended)** - Additional stability, requires 115mm clearance (long) or 81mm (short)
    - **Universal Mount** - Works across many bikes, requires 49mm clearance
    - **Ultra Short Mod** - For bikes with less than 49mm clearance
    
    **Skill Level:** Intermediate (basic soldering required)
    
    **Required Clearance:** 49mm minimum (Universal), up to 115mm (Direct Mount Long)
    
    [Learn more about V2 →](/hardware/v2-through-hole)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="V1 - Archive">
    ### V1 - Archive
    
    The original printable version, now archived.
    
    <Warning>
      V1 is no longer recommended for new builds. The V1 folder is maintained only for printing replacement parts for existing V1 builds.
    </Warning>
    
    **Limitations:**
    - Common assets (gears, mounts, inserts) are not compatible
    - Larger, more complex prints
    - Less stable mounting system
    - Requires V1-specific knob cups and adaptors
    
    **When to Use V1:**
    - Only if you need replacement parts for an existing V1 build
    
    **Recommendation:** Build V2 or V3 instead for better performance and easier assembly.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Common Components

All V2 and V3 builds share these common components:

### Required 3D Printed Parts

- **Gears** - 11 tooth and 40 tooth internal drive gears
- **Inserts** - Bike-specific knob adaptors (40+ bike models supported)
- **Knob Cup** - Retains insert and connects to internal gearbox
- **Arm** - Connects SmartSpin2k to bike mount (V2 Direct Mount & V3 only)
- **Bike Mount** - Attaches to head tube (V2 Direct Mount & V3 only)
- **Shifters** - TPU shifters for manual resistance control

## Choosing Your Version

<Accordion title="I want the easiest build with minimal soldering">
  **Choose V3 Integrated PCB**
  
  The V3 version uses a manufactured PCB that arrives fully assembled. You just need to:
  - 3D print the case and accessories
  - Plug in the cables
  - Mount to your bike
  
  Perfect for beginners or those who want a quick, reliable build.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="I have basic soldering skills and want to save money">
  **Choose V2 Through Hole**
  
  The V2 version uses readily available components that you solder yourself. Benefits:
  - Lower cost than V3
  - Easy hand-soldering with through-hole components
  - Multiple case options for different bikes
  - Well-documented and widely built by the community
  
  Requires a soldering iron and basic electronics experience.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="I have a Peloton bike">
  **Choose V3 Integrated PCB**
  
  Only V3 has the UART to serial driver specifically designed for direct integration with Peloton bikes. This provides:
  - Native Peloton communication
  - Better resistance control
  - Direct serial interface support
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="My bike has limited clearance space">
  **Measure your clearance first:**
  
  1. Measure from the back of the head tube to the center of the knob
  2. Choose based on available space:
     - **Less than 49mm**: V2 Ultra Short Mod
     - **49-80mm**: V2 Universal or Direct Mount Short
     - **80mm+**: V2 Direct Mount Long or V3
     - **115mm+**: V2 Direct Mount Long or V3
</Accordion>
