# 3D Printing Guide

This guide covers all 3D printed components for SmartSpin2k, including print settings, materials, and tips for successful prints.

## Overview

SmartSpin2k requires several 3D printed components depending on your hardware version and bike model. All STL files are available in the [Hardware directory](https://github.com/doudar/SmartSpin2k/tree/develop/Hardware).

### Required Prints (All Versions)

- **Case** - Houses electronics and gearbox
- **Gears** - 11T and 40T drive gears (11:40 ratio)
- **Knob Cup** - Retains insert and connects to gearbox
- **Insert** - Bike-specific knob adaptor

### Additional Prints (Version Dependent)

- **Arm** - Connects SS2k to bike mount (V2 Direct Mount & V3)
- **Bike Mount** - Attaches to head tube (V2 Direct Mount & V3)
- **Shifters** - Manual resistance controls (optional, all versions)
- **Mounting Strap** - For V2 Universal Mount

### Gears

**Files Location:** `Hardware/Common Assets/Gears/`

**Required Parts:**
- 11T spur gear: `Spur gear 1M 11T.STL`
- 40T spur gear: `SS2K_40T_Mod1_Spur_Gear.STL` (plastic shaft) OR `40T HEX` (for metal 5/16" bolt shaft)

**Print Settings:**
- Material: PLA or PETG (PETG preferred)
- Layer height: 0.15-0.2mm
- **Infill: 100%** (critical!)
- Perimeters: 4-5
- Speed: 40-50mm/s (slower for accuracy)
- No supports needed

**Tips:**
- Orient gears flat on bed
- Use glue stick for bed adhesion
- Let cool completely before removing
- Test gear mesh before installing in case

**HEX Shaft Option:**
- Print `40T HEX` version if using metal shaft
- Requires 5/16" bolt, washers, and nuts
- More durable than plastic shaft
- Apply Loctite to nuts after assembly

### Knob Cups

**Files Location:** `Hardware/Common Assets/KnobCups/`

**Options:**
- **Standard Knob Cup** - Most common, plastic shaft
- **Knob Cup HEX** - For metal 5/16" bolt shaft

**Print Settings:**
- Material: PLA or PETG
- Infill: 40%
- Perimeters: 4
- No supports needed

**Assembly:**
1. Print knob cup
2. Print insert separately (bike-specific)
3. Insert slides into knob cup
4. For custom builds, combine in slicer and print as one piece

### Arm

**Files Location:** `Hardware/Common Assets/Arm/`

**Available Lengths:** 20mm to 165mm in 5mm increments

**Choosing Arm Length:**
1. Measure from back of head tube to center of knob
2. Subtract 65mm
3. Result is your required arm length
4. See [arm sizing table](/hardware/v3-integrated-pcb#arm-length-selection)

**Print Settings:**
- Material: PETG or PLA (PETG recommended)
- Infill: 40-50%
- Perimeters: 4
- Layer height: 0.2mm

**Custom Lengths:**
- [Thingiverse Customizer](https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:5732525)
- Adjust length parameter to exact measurement
- Export and print custom STL

**Components:**
- Arm body (various lengths)
- Arm hook (_arm hook.stl) - mandatory

### Shifters

**Files Location:** `Hardware/Common Assets/Shifters/`

**Components:**
- strap.stl - Main strap body (TPU)
- plug.stl - x2 (TPU)
- retainer.stl - (TPU)
- test_housing.stl - Test print (TPU)
- shifter_set.stl - Complete set on one plate

**Material:** TPU (95A durometer)

**Print Settings:**

| Setting | Value |
|---------|-------|
| Layer height | 0.2mm |
| Nozzle | 0.4mm |
| Perimeters | 3 |
| Infill | 20% |
| Infill angle | 90° (along strap length) |
| Top/bottom pattern | Monotonic |
| Cooling | 75% (adjust for printer) |
| Speed | Slow (20-30mm/s) |
| Supports | Disabled |

**Height Range Modifier (8.85-13.70mm):**
- Solid infill pattern: Ironing
- Top infill pattern: Concentric
- Creates watertight seal against sweat

**Assembly:**
1. Print all parts in TPU
2. Install momentary microswitches
3. Hot glue switches in place
4. Connect RCA to 3.5mm audio cable
5. Seal with hot glue

**Testing:**
- Print test_housing.stl first
- Verifies bridging layers are sealed
- Uses less material and time
- Dial in settings before full print

<Warning>
  TPU bridging is tricky. Good cooling and slow speed are essential for watertight prints.
</Warning>

## Material Guide

### PLA (Polylactic Acid)

**Pros:**
- Easiest to print
- Good detail and surface finish
- Widely available
- Low cost
- Biodegradable

**Cons:**
- Lower heat resistance (~60°C)
- More brittle than PETG
- Can creep under sustained load

**Best For:**
- Case parts (if not in hot environment)
- Inserts and knob cups
- Testing and prototypes

### PETG (Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol)

**Pros:**
- More durable than PLA
- Better heat resistance (~80°C)
- More flexible, less brittle
- Good layer adhesion
- Chemical resistant

**Cons:**
- More stringing than PLA
- Requires higher temps
- More prone to moisture absorption

**Best For:**
- Gears (wear resistance)
- Arms and mounts (load-bearing)
- Cases in warm environments

### ABS (Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene)

**Pros:**
- High strength
- Good heat resistance (~100°C)
- Can be acetone smoothed
- Durable

**Cons:**
- Warping issues
- Requires enclosure
- Unpleasant fumes
- Harder to print

**Best For:**
- High-temperature environments
- Maximum durability required

### TPU (Thermoplastic Polyurethane)

**Pros:**
- Flexible and shock-absorbing
- Excellent wear resistance
- Good grip

**Cons:**
- Difficult to print
- Requires slow speeds
- Prone to stringing
- Can't use high retraction

**Best For:**
- Shifters only (required)
- Optional: inserts for irregular knobs

## Troubleshooting

<Accordion title="Gears are noisy or skip">
  **Possible Causes:**
  - Insufficient infill (must be 100%)
  - Under-extrusion creating weak teeth
  - Poor layer adhesion
  - Incorrect gear mesh alignment
  
  **Solutions:**
  - Reprint gears at 100% infill
  - Calibrate extruder e-steps
  - Increase nozzle temperature slightly
  - Check gear alignment in assembly
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Case parts don't fit together">
  **Possible Causes:**
  - Printer dimensional accuracy issues
  - Warping during print
  - Supports not removed fully
  - Wrong filament type (shrinkage)
  
  **Solutions:**
  - Calibrate printer (XYZ steps, flow rate)
  - Use brim for better bed adhesion
  - Clean support material completely
  - Try PLA if using ABS (less shrinkage)
  - Light sanding of mating surfaces
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Shifter strap not watertight">
  **Possible Causes:**
  - Bridging layers have gaps
  - Insufficient cooling
  - Print speed too fast
  - Missing height range modifier settings
  
  **Solutions:**
  - Print test_housing.stl first
  - Increase cooling to 75%+
  - Slow down to 20-30mm/s
  - Apply ironing and concentric top fill at 8.85-13.70mm
  - Use monotonic infill pattern
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Insert doesn't fit knob">
  **Possible Causes:**
  - Wrong insert model selected
  - Printer over-extrusion
  - Knob dimensions vary from design
  
  **Solutions:**
  - Try alternate size (most bikes have 2 versions)
  - Calibrate flow rate / extrusion multiplier
  - Measure knob and compare to Design_Reference_Photos
  - Scale insert in slicer (95-105% typically safe)
  - Print in TPU for flexible fit
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Arm or mount breaking under stress">
  **Possible Causes:**
  - Insufficient perimeters/walls
  - Low infill percentage
  - Layer adhesion issues
  - Wrong material (PLA creep)
  
  **Solutions:**
  - Use PETG or ABS instead of PLA
  - Increase perimeters to 4-5
  - Increase infill to 50%
  - Increase nozzle temp for better layer bonding
  - Orient print for strength (layers perpendicular to stress)
</Accordion>

## Pre-Print Checklist

- [ ] Downloaded correct STL files for your version (V2/V3)
- [ ] Selected appropriate bike-specific insert
- [ ] Chosen correct arm length (if using Direct Mount)
- [ ] Selected bike mount model (if using Direct Mount)
- [ ] Calibrated printer (bed level, e-steps, flow)
- [ ] Verified filament is dry (especially PETG, TPU)
- [ ] Sliced with correct settings (infill, perimeters)
- [ ] Checked gears are set to 100% infill
- [ ] Previewed layers in slicer for issues
- [ ] Prepared bed surface (glue stick, tape, etc.)

## Post-Processing Tips

1. **Remove Supports Carefully** - Use flush cutters, don't force
2. **Clean Up Stringing** - Heat gun or lighter (carefully)
3. **Sand Mating Surfaces** - 220 grit for smooth fit
4. **Test Fit Before Assembly** - Ensure all parts fit together
5. **Heat-Set Inserts** - 200-220°C, press gently and straight
6. **Dry Fit Screws** - Check thread engagement before final assembly
