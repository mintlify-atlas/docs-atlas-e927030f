# V2 - Through Hole

The V2 Through Hole version is designed with DIY builders in mind, featuring hand-solderable components on a carrier board PCB and multiple case options for different bike configurations.

<img src="/Pictures/Version2.0.jpg" alt="V2 SmartSpin2k" />

## Overview

V2 is the DIY-friendly version that prints easier, is smaller, and uses less hardware than V1. It features a much better 11:40 tooth gear ratio and supports a carrier board PCB onto which development boards mount for easy wiring and soldering.

### Key Features

- **Hand-Solderable PCB** - Through-hole components anyone can solder
- **Multiple Case Options** - Direct Mount, Universal, and Ultra Short variants
- **Improved Gear Ratio** - 11:40 tooth gearing for optimal torque
- **Smaller Footprint** - Compact design that fits more bikes
- **Carrier Board Design** - Devkits mount directly for easy wiring
- **Community Proven** - Widely built and documented

## Case Options

V2 offers three main case configurations to suit different bike geometries:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Direct Mount (Recommended)">
    ### Direct Mount
    
    The recommended case design providing maximum stability, especially on bikes with spherical knobs.
    
    **Features:**
    - Rigid mounting to bike head tube
    - One-handed installation and removal
    - Superior stability compared to universal mount
    - Available in Long and Short versions
    
    **Requirements:**
    - Bike-specific mount from Common Assets
    - Arm component (various lengths available)
    - M3 heat-set inserts
    - M3 and M5 screws
    
    **Clearance Requirements:**
    - **Long Case:** 115mm minimum (Bowflex C6, Peloton, Echelon Connect Sport)
    - **Short Case:** 81mm minimum (Bowflex C7)
    
    **Tested Bikes:**
    - Bowflex C6 / Schwinn IC4 (85mm arm)
    - Bowflex C7 (45mm arm, short case)
    - Echelon Connect Sport (105mm arm)
    - Peloton Bike (75mm arm, 90mm for slammed bars)
    
    **Additional Components:**
    - M3 x 5 x 4mm heat-set inserts (5mm diameter)
    - 4x M3 screws (M3x8 or M3x10)
    - 1x M5x12mm screw (arm connection)
    - 2x M5x30mm screws (bike mount and case)
    - 3x M5 nuts
    - Velcro strap (1/2" x 8") OR VHB tape
    - 2mm non-slip adhesive rubber (optional)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Universal Mount">
    ### Universal Mount
    
    Suitable for use across numerous bikes or when a direct mount solution is not required.
    
    **Features:**
    - Works on many different bike models
    - Strap-based mounting system
    - No bike-specific parts needed
    - Simpler assembly
    
    **Requirements:**
    - Mounting strap (print from Universal Mount directory)
    - #8 general purpose wood screws (4 pieces)
    - Standard V2 case (left and right)
    
    **Clearance Requirements:**
    - **Minimum:** 49mm between center of knob and rear of head tube
    
    **Components:**
    - Body_Left.stl
    - Body_Right.stl
    - Mounting_For_New_Case.STL
    - Knob_Cup_Large_HEX.STL (or standard)
    - Bike-specific insert
    - 11T and 40T gears
    
    **Assembly:**
    Uses a HEX shaft design with 5/16" bolt for metal output shaft option.
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Ultra Short Mod">
    ### Ultra Short Mod
    
    A modified version of the standard V2 case shortened to fit bikes with very limited clearance.
    
    **Features:**
    - Shortened case design
    - Fits bikes under 49mm clearance
    - Same electronics as standard V2
    - Maintains 11:40 gear ratio
    
    **When to Use:**
    - Bikes with less than 49mm head tube spacing
    - Very compact bike geometries
    - When space is at an absolute premium
    
    <Warning>
      The Ultra Short Mod requires very precise printing and assembly. Test fit all parts before final assembly.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Assembly Instructions

<Accordion title="Step 1: Solder the PCB">
  **Using the Soldering Jig (Recommended):**
  
  1. Place PCB in soldering jig
  2. Insert all through-hole components
  3. Ensure components are flush and aligned
  4. Solder all connections from bottom of PCB
  5. Inspect for cold joints and bridges
  6. Test continuity with multimeter
  
  **Component Order:**
  1. Resistors (flat components first)
  2. Diodes (mind polarity)
  3. Capacitors (electrolytic - mind polarity)
  4. Sockets for ICs (if using)
  5. Connectors and headers
  6. Mount devkits (ESP32, TMC2209)
  
  <Warning>
    Double-check polarity on diodes, electrolytic capacitors, and LEDs before soldering.
  </Warning>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Step 2: Test Electronics">
  Before installing in case:
  
  1. Connect power supply (12-24V)
  2. Check ESP32 powers on (LED indicator)
  3. Flash firmware via USB
  4. Test stepper motor connection
  5. Verify stepper motor responds to commands
  6. Test shifter inputs (if using)
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Step 3: Print All Parts">
  **Print Settings:**
  - Layer height: 0.2mm
  - Nozzle: 0.4mm (0.6mm acceptable)
  - Infill: 40%
  - Perimeters: 4
  - Supports: From bed only (case halves)
  
  **Materials:**
  - Case parts: PLA, PETG, or ABS
  - Gears: PLA or PETG (solid infill recommended)
  - Shifters: TPU (flexible material)
  
  See [3D Printing Guide](/hardware/3d-printing) for detailed settings.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Step 4: Assemble Gearbox">
  **Universal Mount (HEX Shaft):**
  
  <img src="/Pictures/Axel_Assembly.gif" alt="Axle assembly" />
  
  1. Install 40T HEX gear on 5/16" bolt
  2. Add washers and nuts on both sides
  3. Tighten and ensure smooth rotation
  4. Apply blue or red Loctite to nuts
  5. Install 11T gear on stepper motor shaft
  6. Insert bike-specific insert into knob cup
  7. Mount knob cup to 40T gear shaft
  
  **Direct Mount:**
  
  1. Use 40T gear with integrated plastic shaft OR HEX version
  2. Install insert in knob cup
  3. Mount 11T gear to stepper motor
  4. Test gear mesh before final assembly
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Step 5: Install in Case">
  <img src="/Pictures/Case_Assembly.gif" alt="Case assembly" />
  
  **For Direct Mount (with heat-set inserts):**
  1. Install M3 heat-set inserts in case mounting holes
  2. Mount PCB and stepper motor in right case half
  3. Route wires neatly
  4. Install gearbox assembly
  5. Close left case half
  6. Secure with M3 screws
  
  **For Universal Mount:**
  1. Mount PCB and stepper motor
  2. Install gearbox
  3. Close case halves
  4. Secure with #8 wood screws (4 places)
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Step 6: Mount to Bike">
  **Direct Mount Installation:**
  1. Choose and print correct bike mount
  2. Attach mount to head tube with velcro or VHB tape
  3. Install arm with M5x30mm screw
  4. Connect SmartSpin2k to arm with M5x12mm screw
  5. Align with resistance knob
  6. Test operation
  
  **Universal Mount Installation:**
  1. Position SmartSpin2k on bike
  2. Use mounting strap to secure to frame
  3. Align knob cup with resistance knob
  4. Adjust position and tighten
  5. Test resistance control
</Accordion>

## Troubleshooting

<Accordion title="Stepper motor gets hot">
  - Check stepper driver current settings
  - Verify TMC2209 Vref voltage
  - Ensure adequate cooling
  - Check for mechanical binding in gears
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Gears skipping or noisy">
  - Verify 11:40 tooth gear mesh alignment
  - Check for proper gear spacing
  - Ensure gears are printed with adequate infill
  - Lubricate with dry lubricant if needed
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Resistance control erratic">
  - Test power supply voltage under load
  - Check all solder joints for cold joints
  - Verify ESP32 is receiving stable power
  - Recalibrate in SmartSpin2k software
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can't upload firmware">
  - Install CP2102 USB drivers
  - Try different USB cable
  - Hold BOOT button during upload
  - Check COM port in Arduino IDE/PlatformIO
</Accordion>

## Customization Options

### Custom Arm Lengths

If standard arm lengths don't fit your bike:
- [Thingiverse Customizer](https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:5732525)
- Adjust arm length to your exact measurement
- Export and print custom length

### Custom Bike Mount

If your bike isn't in the library:
- [OpenSCAD Customizer](https://www.thingiverse.com/thing:5738735)
- Measure yoke width of your head tube
- Adjust parameters and export STL

### Alternative Materials

- **Cases:** Try PETG or ABS for higher temperature resistance
- **Gears:** Print at 100% infill for maximum strength
- **Inserts:** Use TPU for bikes with irregular knob shapes
